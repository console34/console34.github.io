Conference

Going Romance is a European conference series that focuses on Romance languages from the perspective of current linguistic theorising and experimental research. Over the past 30 years, it has evolved into a major European discussion forum for theoretically relevant research on Romance languages.

Going Romance 2025 will be hosted by the Department of Linguistics and Comparative Cultural Studies (DSLCC) at Ca’ Foscari University of Venice.

Besides the main sessions, the conference includes a workshop on “Gender in Romance languages”, exploring how formal approaches can inform communication policies.

Call for papers

We encourage submissions of papers at the intersection of linguistic theory and Romance languages. Beyond this general scope, there are no specific restrictions regarding the topic, subdiscipline, theoretical approach or methodology for the main session.
Submissions to the workshop should focus on how theoretical approaches to gender can inform inclusive communication policies in the different Romance languages.

Presentations will be 20 minutes long, followed by a 10-minute discussion period. 
The call is published on Linguist List websiste.
 

Submission guidelines

Abstracts should be written in English and no longer than two pages A4, including references, examples and any relevant figures or tables, with 2.5 cm margins, font size 12, single-spaced. The file must be anonymous in both the abstract and the filename. 

Submissions are limited to a maximum of one individual and one joint abstract per author (or two joint abstracts) for the entire conference. Please note that no changes in authorship will be allowed after submission.

Deadlines:

  • Abstract submission deadline: June 20th, 2025
  • Notification of acceptance: July 31st, 2025

For submission, please send your abstract in PDF format to goingromance2025@unive.it
The subject line of the mail should be: GoingRomance 2025 Abstract Submission.
In the e-mail, please provide:

  • title of the paper, name and surname of the author(s), affiliation, email address;
  • whether your abstract is to be considered for the Main sessions or the Workshop;
  • whether your paper is to be considered for Oral or Poster presentation.

Personal data associated with these cookies is: User IP, browser language, average visit time, page actions (downloads, outlinks), first visit, last visit, device used, ecc.  The legal basis for this processing activity is represented by art. 6.1.e) of the Regulation (“execution of a task of public interest or connected to the exercise of public powers''), as the University aims at collecting statistical data on the visits of its Website pages especially with regards to the pages of “Amministrazione Trasparente”. Is not possible to disable these cookies through the banner, as this action will have a negative effect on your navigation of the Website.
